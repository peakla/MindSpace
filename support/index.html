How can we help you?

At MindBalance, we believe in being open and supportive. Find answers to your questions about mental health, therapy, and getting help.

01
Crisis Support Is the 988 Lifeline really free?
Yes, the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline is completely free and available 24/7. You can call or text 988 from anywhere in the United States. The service is confidential and staffed by trained crisis counselors. You can also text 988 if you prefer not to call. Both options connect you to the same trained counselors.
02
Crisis Support What happens when I call a crisis line?
When you call a crisis line, you'll be connected with a trained counselor who will listen without judgment. They'll help you work through what you're feeling, provide support, and help you create a plan to stay safe. You don't need to be suicidal to call - any mental health crisis is valid. Crisis lines are for anyone in distress, not just those in immediate danger. Don't hesitate to reach out if you're struggling.
03
Therapy & Treatment How do I know if I need professional help?
Consider seeking professional help if your feelings are affecting your daily life, relationships, work, or school. Signs include persistent sadness, anxiety, changes in sleep or appetite, difficulty concentrating, or thoughts of self-harm. Remember: seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Trust your instincts. If you feel like something isn't right, talking to a professional can help you understand and address it.
04
Therapy & Treatment Are counseling sessions confidential?
Yes, all counseling sessions are confidential. Mental health professionals are bound by strict privacy laws and ethical guidelines. The only exceptions are if there's an immediate risk of harm to yourself or others, or in cases involving abuse of minors or vulnerable adults. You can ask your therapist about their specific confidentiality policies during your first session.
05
Resources & Costs What if I can't afford therapy?
There are many low-cost or free mental health options available. Community mental health centers often offer sliding-scale fees based on income. Many therapists offer reduced rates, and some nonprofits provide free counseling. Crisis lines like 988 are always free, and support groups can be a valuable no-cost resource. Check with your insurance, school, or employer - many offer mental health benefits you may not know about.
06
Coping & Self-Help How can I help a friend who's struggling?
Listen without judgment and let them know you care. Avoid trying to "fix" their problems - sometimes just being present is enough. Encourage them to seek professional help, and offer to help them find resources or accompany them to an appointment. Take any mention of self-harm seriously and don't be afraid to involve a trusted adult or crisis line if needed. Sometimes the best thing you can do is just be there. Your presence matters more than having the perfect words.
07
Therapy & Treatment What's the difference between a therapist, psychologist, and psychiatrist?
Therapists and counselors provide talk therapy and may have various credentials (LCSW, LPC, MFT). Psychologists have doctoral degrees and can provide therapy and psychological testing. Psychiatrists are medical doctors who can prescribe medication. Many people work with a combination - for example, a therapist for regular sessions and a psychiatrist for medication management. Start with a therapist for talk therapy. They can refer you to a psychiatrist if medication might be helpful.
08
Therapy & Treatment Is it normal to feel nervous about starting therapy?
Absolutely! It's very common to feel anxious about opening up to a stranger. A good therapist will understand this and help you feel comfortable at your own pace. Remember, you're in control of what you share and when. If after a few sessions you don't feel a connection, it's okay to try a different therapist - finding the right fit is important. Write down what you want to discuss before your first session. It can help ease the nervousness.
09
Therapy & Treatment How long does therapy usually take?
It varies greatly depending on your goals and situation. Some people benefit from short-term therapy (8-12 sessions) for specific issues like anxiety or stress management. Others may continue longer for deeper work or ongoing support. Your therapist will work with you to set goals and regularly assess your progress. There's no "right" length for therapy. Some people find value in ongoing sessions, while others prefer focused, time-limited treatment.
10
Coping & Self-Help What should I do if I'm having a panic attack?
Focus on your breathing - try the 4-7-8 technique (breathe in for 4 seconds, hold for 7, exhale for 8). Ground yourself by naming 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, and 1 you can taste. Remind yourself that panic attacks are temporary and not dangerous. If panic attacks are recurring, consider speaking with a mental health professional. Panic attacks usually peak within 10 minutes and subside within 20-30 minutes. Knowing this can help reduce fear during an attack.
